# Style Rubric: Warm Academic Exposition

> Score writing samples 1-5 on how well they match this voice.

## Dimensions

### 1. Pedagogical Structure

Does the writing follow the motivation-intuition-definition-example-theorem-proof-reflection sequence?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | No recognizable pedagogical structure. Concepts appear without motivation or examples. Definitions are dropped without context. |
| 2 | Some structure present but inconsistent. Definitions may precede motivation, or examples are missing for key concepts. |
| 3 | Clear structure with motivation before definitions and examples after them, but proof analysis/reflection is absent. Forward and backward references are sparse. |
| 4 | Strong adherence to the full sequence. Most concepts are motivated, defined, exemplified, and reflected upon. Transitions signal where the reader is in the argument. |
| 5 | Exemplary. Every concept follows the complete sequence naturally. Proof strategies and analyses appear where helpful. The reader always knows why a topic matters before encountering its formal treatment. |

### 2. Tone and Warmth

Does the writing achieve the conversational-academic balance with genuine mentoring warmth?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Either entirely dry/clinical or inappropriately casual. No sense of a human author. No acknowledgment of the reader's experience. |
| 2 | Some personality shows through, but the tone is uneven — lurching between formal and informal. Reader is addressed but not genuinely engaged. |
| 3 | Consistent semi-formal tone. Uses "we" appropriately. Shows some warmth but may feel slightly generic or impersonal in places. |
| 4 | Warm, encouraging, and authoritative. The reader feels guided by a knowledgeable mentor. Difficulty is acknowledged honestly. Parenthetical asides and cultural references add personality. |
| 5 | Indistinguishable from the source. The voice is simultaneously authoritative and approachable — a professor who believes in the student, pushes gently against defeatism, and allows genuine personality to show through subtle humor and cultural literacy. |

### 3. Precision and Definition Discipline

Are technical terms defined before use? Is the language precise without being opaque?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Technical terms used without definition. Language is either vague or impenetrably formal. Symbols appear without explanation. |
| 2 | Some terms defined, but key concepts are assumed or introduced casually. Definitions lack the formal precision of the source style. |
| 3 | Most terms defined on first use with reasonable precision. Bolding convention may be inconsistent. Informal and formal descriptions are present but not always paired. |
| 4 | All terms defined with formal precision, bolded at first occurrence. Concepts are stated informally first, then formally. Notation is introduced deliberately. |
| 5 | Every term is defined exactly when needed — not too early, not too late. The triple restatement (informal, formal, by example) is used for key concepts. The writing is precise without ever being opaque. |

### 4. Sentence Craft and Rhythm

Does the writing exhibit the characteristic sentence-level patterns: medium-long build sentences with short emphatic punches, active voice, and effective use of semicolons and parenthetical asides?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Monotone sentence length. Predominantly passive voice. No rhythmic variation. No parenthetical asides. |
| 2 | Some variation in sentence length but no deliberate rhythm. Active voice used sometimes. Parenthetical asides are absent or awkward. |
| 3 | Reasonable mix of sentence lengths. Active voice predominates. Some effective use of parenthetical asides and semicolons. The build-build-punch pattern appears occasionally. |
| 4 | Strong sentence craft. The build-build-build-punch rhythm is evident. Parenthetical asides feel natural. Semicolons connect related ideas. Short sentences land with emphasis. |
| 5 | Masterful rhythm. Long, clause-rich sentences build the argument methodically, then a short sentence lands the point with impact. Parenthetical asides add texture without disrupting flow. Semicolons, colons, and italicized emphasis are deployed with the same frequency and precision as the source. |

### 5. Rhetorical Repertoire

Does the writing use the characteristic transitional phrases, signposting, and rhetorical moves?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | No recognizable rhetorical moves from the source style. Generic academic transitions only. No signposting. |
| 2 | A few characteristic phrases appear ("indeed," "consequently") but feel forced or are used incorrectly. Signposting is weak. |
| 3 | Several characteristic moves are present and used correctly: "we now turn to," "let's consider," "it follows that." Forward/backward references exist. Historical context appears at least once. |
| 4 | Rich rhetorical repertoire. "Indeed," "of course," "to be sure," "observe that," and "we are now prepared to" appear naturally. Historical/biographical context humanizes the content. Rhetorical questions motivate new topics. |
| 5 | The full repertoire is deployed with the same naturalness as the source. Transitions are varied and never formulaic. "We are now prepared to..." signals genuine milestones. Historical anecdotes and cultural references are woven in seamlessly. The reader is guided by explicit signposting without feeling managed. |

## Scoring Guide

- **Total**: Sum of all dimensions (5-25)
- **21-25**: Excellent match — reads as if written by the original authors
- **16-20**: Good match — captures the voice with minor deviations
- **11-15**: Partial match — some elements present but inconsistent
- **6-10**: Weak match — occasional echoes of the style
- **5**: No match — completely different voice
